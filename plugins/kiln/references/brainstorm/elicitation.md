# The Elicitation Methods

Fifty ways to work material that is already on the table — examine it, pressure-test it,
deepen it. Where techniques widen the field, elicitation methods sharpen what's in it.

**The persona reframe, binding for every method below.** Most of these were written as
multi-persona theater. You never perform the personas as content-generating voices — that
would be authoring. Instead you hand the user the chair: "what would your angriest
customer say about this?", "put on the skeptic's hat for a minute — what breaks first?"
The persona is a lens the USER looks through; every judgment reaching the ledger is
theirs. Each method's `output pattern` describes the shape of the path you steer them
along with questions, one per message.

**Selecting a method.** No fixed checkpoints — choose by what the moment needs: the
material's type, its complexity, its risk, its creative charge. When you shift
instruments, say so in one plain sentence — what you're reaching for and why now — so the
user is never disoriented. No banners, no formulas; a sentence.

Categories at a glance: collaboration (10) · advanced (6) · competitive (3) ·
technical (5) · creative (6) · research (3) · risk (5) · core (6) · learning (2) ·
philosophical (2) · retrospective (2).

## collaboration — many chairs, one user

- **Stakeholder Round Table** — seat their stakeholders around one question and ask the
  user to speak each chair in turn. `perspectives > synthesis > alignment`
- **Expert Panel Review** — "if a panel of specialists reviewed this, what would each one
  flag?" `expert views > consensus > recommendations`
- **Debate Club Showdown** — put their two live options in opposition; have them argue
  both sides in earnest before judging. `thesis > antithesis > synthesis`
- **User Persona Focus Group** — have them channel their actual users reacting to the
  idea: first delight, first complaint. `reactions > concerns > priorities`
- **Time Traveler Council** — past-them and future-them advise present-them on the
  decision. `past wisdom > present choice > future impact`
- **Cross-Functional War Room** — walk the same problem as product owner, engineer, and
  designer in turn; the trade-offs live between the chairs.
  `constraints > trade-offs > balanced solution`
- **Mentor and Apprentice** — have them teach it to an imagined beginner; the naive
  questions surface hidden assumptions. `explanation > questions > deeper understanding`
- **Good Cop Bad Cop** — alternate the lens: "what's strongest about this?" then "what's
  weakest?" Keep the rhythm honest. `encouragement > criticism > balanced view`
- **Improv Yes-And** — chain builds without blocking: each of their answers becomes the
  floor of your next question. `idea > build > build > surprising result`
- **Customer Support Theater** — stage the worst support call this product will ever get;
  ask what the complaint reveals and what would prevent it.
  `complaint > investigation > resolution > prevention`

## advanced — reasoning structures

- **Tree of Thoughts** — open several reasoning branches side by side and walk each
  before judging any. `paths > evaluation > selection`
- **Graph of Thoughts** — connect their ideas as a web, not a list: "which of these
  touch? what pattern does the web make?" `nodes > connections > patterns`
- **Thread of Thought** — narrate one continuous line through everything said so far and
  ask where the thread frays. `context > thread > synthesis`
- **Self-Consistency Validation** — approach the same question two or three independent
  ways and compare arrivals; agreement is signal. `approaches > comparison > consensus`
- **Meta-Prompting Analysis** — step off the path and examine it together: "is the way
  we're exploring this the right way to explore it?" `current > analysis > optimization`
- **Reasoning via Planning** — ask what the goal state actually is, then plan backward
  through the world they've described. `model > planning > strategy`

## competitive — adversarial pressure

- **Red Team vs Blue Team** — have them attack their own design, then defend it; each
  successful attack becomes a hardening. `defense > attack > hardening`
- **Shark Tank Pitch** — "pitch it to a skeptic with money — what hole gets poked first?"
  `pitch > challenges > refinement`
- **Code Review Gauntlet** — pass the approach before reviewers of different philosophies
  — the purist, the pragmatist — and let their debate settle standards.
  `reviews > debates > standards`

## technical — engineering lenses

- **Architecture Decision Records** — options on the table, trade-offs explicit, the
  decision and its why said out loud. `options > trade-offs > decision > rationale`
- **Rubber Duck Debugging Evolved** — have them explain it three times: to a child, a
  peer, a specialist. The stumble marks the gap. `simple > detailed > technical > aha`
- **Algorithm Olympics** — race their candidate approaches against the same benchmark
  question and let the measures pick. `implementations > benchmarks > winner`
- **Security Audit Personas** — walk the system as attacker, defender, auditor: what does
  each see first? `vulnerabilities > defenses > compliance`
- **Performance Profiler Panel** — chase the slowness across layers: what do the symptoms
  point to seen as data, as interface, as operations? `symptoms > analysis > optimizations`

## creative — generative workings

- **SCAMPER Method** — the seven lenses as elicitation, one at a time: substitute what?
  combine which? adapt, modify, repurpose, eliminate, reverse. `S > C > A > M > P > E > R`
- **Reverse Engineering** — start at the finished outcome and step backward: "what had to
  be true just before that?" `end state > steps backward > path forward`
- **What If Scenarios** — alternative realities as probes: change one premise, ask what
  follows. `scenarios > implications > insights`
- **Random Input Stimulus** — one unrelated word; their associations; their bridge back.
  `random word > associations > novel ideas`
- **Exquisite Corpse Brainstorm** — build showing only the last line: respond to their
  newest contribution alone and let the surprise compound.
  `contribution > handoff > contribution > surprise`
- **Genre Mashup** — cross their domain with a foreign one and ask what the hybrid
  inherits from each parent. `domain A + domain B > hybrid insights`

## research — evidence discipline

- **Literature Review Personas** — read the evidence three ways: as optimist, as skeptic,
  as synthesizer. `sources > critiques > synthesis`
- **Thesis Defense Simulation** — their idea is the thesis; you convene the committee's
  questions, they defend and refine. `thesis > challenges > defense > refinements`
- **Comparative Analysis Matrix** — options against weighted criteria, scored out loud;
  the matrix recommends, they decide. `options > criteria > scores > recommendation`

## risk — looking for trouble on purpose

- **Pre-mortem Analysis** — "it's a year from now and this failed. Write the obituary —
  what killed it?" Prevention falls out of the causes.
  `failure scenario > causes > prevention`
- **Failure Mode Analysis** — piece by piece: "how does this part die, and what does its
  death take with it?" `components > failures > prevention`
- **Challenge from Critical Perspective** — devil's advocate, announced and time-boxed:
  stress the idea to strengthen it, never to win. `assumptions > challenges > strengthening`
- **Identify Potential Risks** — sweep the categories — technical, human, market, time —
  asking what could go wrong in each. `categories > risks > mitigations`
- **Chaos Monkey Scenarios** — pull a random plank out of the design and ask what
  happens. `break > observe > harden`

## core — the everyday blades

- **First Principles Analysis** — assumptions out, certainties kept, rebuild from what
  remains. `assumptions > truths > new approach`
- **5 Whys Deep Dive** — why, five times, until the answer stops moving.
  `why chain > root cause > solution`
- **Socratic Questioning** — targeted questions that let them discover what they already
  half-know. `questions > revelations > understanding`
- **Critique and Refine** — strengths named first, weaknesses second, then one concrete
  improvement pass. `strengths/weaknesses > improvements > refined`
- **Explain Reasoning** — ask for the steps, not the verdict: "walk me from premise to
  conclusion." `steps > logic > conclusion`
- **Expand or Contract for Audience** — have them retell it for a different audience —
  the boardroom version, the hallway version — and notice what each keeps.
  `audience > adjustments > refined content`

## learning — testing understanding

- **Feynman Technique** — teach it to a child; where the simple words run out, so does
  the understanding. `complex > simple > gaps > mastery`
- **Active Recall Testing** — close the notes: "say back what we've settled so far, from
  memory." The gaps announce themselves. `test > gaps > reinforcement`

## philosophical — first questions

- **Occam's Razor Application** — ask for the simplest telling that still explains
  everything, then shave the rest. `options > simplification > selection`
- **Trolley Problem Variations** — pose the trade-off as a dilemma with real stakes and
  ask which value wins, and why. `dilemma > analysis > decision`

## retrospective — looking back to look forward

- **Hindsight Reflection** — stand in the imagined future and look back: "knowing how it
  went, what do you see now?" `future view > insights > application`
- **Lessons Learned Extraction** — mine an experience they named for takeaways, then
  convert each lesson into a next action. `experience > lessons > actions`
