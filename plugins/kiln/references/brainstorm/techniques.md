# The Technique Library

Sixty-two instruments in ten categories. Each entry carries its phase tag — **frame**
(define the problem), **diverge** (widen the field), **reframe** (change the angle),
**converge** (draw together), **stress** (pressure-test) — and one line of craft: how the
technique runs as pure elicitation, one question per message.

How to play any of these: you supply the lens, the user supplies every idea seen through
it. A stimulus, a persona, a provocation is an optic, never a proposal — if the user asks
you to make the connection for them, that is a spark request, and the one-spark law
applies. Confirm each technique you pick by name plus one line of what it does, so the
user knows what game is on the table; never recite this library at them.

Categories at a glance: collaborative (5) · creative (11) · deep (8) ·
introspective_delight (6) · structured (7) · theatrical (6) · wild (8) · biomimetic (3) ·
quantum (3) · cultural (5).

## collaborative — the energy of a room

These were written for groups. Your room holds one user, so the "others" are the user's
own earlier lines, the perspectives they inhabit, and you as welcoming scribe — never you
as contributor.

- **Yes And Building** (diverge) — greet each idea as a launching pad: "yes — and where
  could that go next?" Your welcome is the momentum; every addition stays theirs.
- **Brain Writing Round Robin** (diverge) — invite an uninterrupted burst: let them pour
  out lines without response, then walk back through their own list together asking which
  entries want building.
- **Random Stimulation** (reframe) — offer one far-away word or image as a catalyst and
  ask what it touches in their problem. The bridge is theirs to build.
- **Role Playing** (frame) — ask them to stand where a stakeholder stands: "what does the
  night-shift admin want from this? what would they curse at?"
- **Ideation Relay Race** (diverge) — thirty-second bursts against the clock: quick,
  unpolished, next. Time pressure mutes the inner editor.

## creative — lateral moves

- **What If Scenarios** (reframe) — un-nail the constraints one at a time: "what if
  resources were unlimited? what if the opposite were true? what if this problem simply
  didn't exist?"
- **Analogical Thinking** (reframe) — "this is like... what?" Let them name the parallel,
  then mine it: what does that domain do that this one hasn't tried?
- **Reversal Inversion** (reframe) — flip it: "what would the opposite approach look
  like?" The hidden assumption is whatever the flip exposes.
- **First Principles Thinking** (frame) — strip to bedrock: "what do you know for certain
  here? if you started from scratch, what would you keep?"
- **Forced Relationships** (diverge) — hand them two unrelated things from their own
  transcript and ask what bridge could join them.
- **Time Shifting** (reframe) — move the problem across eras: "how was this solved in
  1970? what does the 2100 version look like?" Another era's constraints expose this
  era's accidents.
- **Metaphor Mapping** (reframe) — when they reach for a metaphor, extend it with them:
  "if the app is a garden, what's the compost? who's the gardener?" Map elements until an
  insight falls out.
- **Cross-Pollination** (diverge) — "how would a hospital run this? a game studio? an
  airline?" Other industries are answer libraries; the borrowing is theirs to do.
- **Concept Blending** (converge) — take two of their strongest threads and ask what new
  thing exists only when they merge — a blend that transcends, not just combines.
- **Reverse Brainstorming** (stress) — invite problems instead of solutions: "how could we
  guarantee this fails?" Then walk the wreckage backward together.
- **Sensory Exploration** (diverge) — ask what the thing feels, sounds, even smells like
  in use. The senses reach places requirements language can't name.

## deep — root systems

- **Five Whys** (frame) — follow each answer with why, gently, until bedrock. The fifth
  why lands somewhere the first would never have guessed.
- **Morphological Analysis** (converge) — have them name the problem's parameters and the
  options under each, then walk combinations they've never tried.
- **Provocation Technique** (reframe) — have them plant one deliberately absurd claim about
  the thing ("what's the most impossible thing you could declare true here?") and ask what
  useful principle survives it.
- **Assumption Reversal** (reframe) — surface their load-bearing assumptions, then flip
  one: "if that weren't true, what would you build instead?"
- **Question Storming** (frame) — questions only for a stretch, answers forbidden: "what
  SHOULD we be asking here?" A well-shaped question is half the vision.
- **Constraint Mapping** (frame) — inventory every constraint, then sort together: which
  are physics and which are habit? The imagined ones are doors.
- **Failure Analysis** (stress) — ask for failures they've watched: "what killed the last
  attempt at this? which lesson is still unpaid?"
- **Emergent Thinking** (converge) — stop steering; ask what pattern the last stretch of
  ideas wants to become. Name what's trying to emerge only to let them confirm or correct
  it.

## introspective_delight — inward, warmly

- **Inner Child Conference** (reframe) — "what would seven-year-old you ask about this?"
  Why, why, why — boring thinking forbidden.
- **Shadow Work Mining** (frame) — "what part of this have you been avoiding?" Resistance
  marks buried treasure; dig gently.
- **Values Archaeology** (frame) — dig to what actually matters: "why do you care about
  this one? what's non-negotiable?" Bedrock values decide ties later.
- **Future Self Interview** (reframe) — "you're eighty, looking back at this project —
  what do you tell yourself today?"
- **Body Wisdom Dialogue** (diverge) — "where do you feel this idea — excitement or a
  knot?" The gut files reports the mind hasn't read yet.
- **Permission Giving** (diverge) — grant it out loud: "impossible is allowed for the next
  ten minutes." Self-imposed limits fall to explicit permission.

## structured — scaffolding

- **SCAMPER Method** (converge) — walk their concept through seven doors, one question
  each: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, Reverse.
- **Six Thinking Hats** (frame) — tour the perspectives one hat at a time: facts,
  feelings, benefits, risks, fresh ideas, process. Ask which hat they've been skipping.
- **Mind Mapping** (diverge) — spoken cartography: center concept, then "what branches off
  that? and off that?" Read the map back so they can see it.
- **Resource Constraints** (stress) — impose an extreme: "one dollar, no technology, ship
  in an hour — what survives?" What survives is the essence.
- **Decision Tree Mapping** (converge) — walk the forks: "if you choose X, which door
  closes? where does each path end up?"
- **Solution Matrix** (converge) — cross their variables against their approaches out
  loud and hunt the empty cells; gaps are where ideas hide.
- **Trait Transfer** (reframe) — "name something great and unrelated. What trait makes it
  great? What would that trait look like here?"

## theatrical — staged perspective

- **Time Travel Talk Show** (reframe) — host the interview: past self, present self,
  future self on one couch. Ask who disagrees with whom.
- **Alien Anthropologist** (reframe) — "an alien watches your users for a day — what
  baffles it?" The familiar turns strange, and assumptions become visible.
- **Dream Fusion Laboratory** (diverge) — start impossible: "describe the fantasy version,
  physics aside." Then reverse-engineer together: what bridging step exists today?
- **Emotion Orchestra** (diverge) — let one emotion at a time conduct: "what does the
  angry version want? the fearful one? the hopeful one?" Then ask how the sections
  harmonize.
- **Parallel Universe Cafe** (reframe) — change one law of their reality — physics, norms,
  history — and ask what they'd build there. Then ask what smuggles back to this universe.
- **Persona Journey** (frame) — have them wear an archetype — explorer, guardian,
  trickster — and ask what that character sees that plain eyes miss.

## wild — controlled demolition

- **Chaos Engineering** (stress) — break it on purpose, in conversation: "everything that
  can fail does. What still stands? What do you rebuild first?"
- **Guerrilla Gardening Ideas** (diverge) — "where's the least expected place this could
  take root?" Stealth placement finds channels the front door misses.
- **Pirate Code Brainstorm** (diverge) — "what would you steal, no permission asked, and
  from whom?" Remix beats reverence; the loot list is theirs.
- **Zombie Apocalypse Planning** (stress) — collapse everything: "society's gone,
  resources are rubble — what part of this still matters?" Survival mode names core
  value.
- **Drunk History Retelling** (reframe) — "explain it like you're three drinks in — no
  filter, no jargon." The uninhibited version is often the true one.
- **Anti-Solution** (stress) — "how would you sabotage this project from the inside?"
  Every sabotage route marks a real vulnerability.
- **Quantum Superposition** (converge) — hold their contradictory favorites side by side
  without choosing; ask what each contributes, and what observation would finally force
  the choice.
- **Elemental Forces** (diverge) — "how would water solve this — flow around it? what
  would fire burn away?" Primal verbs unlock moves polite analysis won't make.

## biomimetic — nature's patent library

- **Nature's Solutions** (reframe) — "what in nature already solves this?" 3.8 billion
  years of R&D, free to read.
- **Ecosystem Thinking** (frame) — ask what the system's species are: who feeds whom,
  what's symbiotic, what succession comes next.
- **Evolutionary Pressure** (converge) — "if only the fittest version survives, what
  pressure does the selecting? what adapts, what goes extinct?"

## quantum — strange but useful

- **Observer Effect** (reframe) — "who's watching this system, and how does being watched
  change it?" Measurement is a design force.
- **Entanglement Thinking** (converge) — "which far-apart pieces of this move together?
  Touch one — what else trembles?"
- **Superposition Collapse** (converge) — keep every option alive until a real constraint
  arrives, then ask which option the constraint chooses.

## cultural — old wisdom, new problems

- **Indigenous Wisdom** (reframe) — "how would a tradition that has survived a thousand
  years hold this problem?" Ancestral systems solved durability long before software.
- **Fusion Cuisine** (diverge) — mix two cultural approaches and ask what hybrid appears —
  the flavor neither kitchen had alone.
- **Ritual Innovation** (frame) — "what would make using this feel ceremonial? what
  transformation does it mark?"
- **Mythic Frameworks** (frame) — "what myth is this project living? who's the hero, and
  what's the threshold?" Archetypes organize what lists can't.
- **Ceremony Prototyping** (converge) — rehearse the solution as a repeatable ritual: who
  participates, what signals commitment, where does the ritual break down?
